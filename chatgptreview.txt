Overall Assessment
Category	Rating	Notes
Character consistency	9.5/10	Strong fidelity; Nick's deeper emotional complexity and the narrator's uncanny familiarity with Sionna could be emphasized more.
Plot chronology	8.5/10	The opening follows the book well, but the library/Wuthering Heights sequence is moved ahead of the Joe's Bar scene, disrupting the original cause-and-effect.
Setting consistency	8/10	Winter atmosphere is generally excellent, but Scene 2's "summer afternoon" conflicts with the novel's consistently cold Milwaukee setting.
Dialogue fidelity	9.5/10	Captures the narrator's voice and the characters' speech patterns exceptionally well, with only occasional over-polished narration.
Highest-priority corrections
Restore the original chronology: Ma's confusion → Joe's Bar → Nick conversation → library → Wuthering Heights → dream.
Remove the "summer afternoon" description from Scene 2 and keep the setting consistent with the novel's winter environment.
Reinforce the narrator's immediate sense that he has "known" Sionna before, as this becomes an important psychological thread later in the story.
Broaden Nick's characterization beyond being "quick to anger" to reflect his combination of dominance, protectiveness, generosity, and volatility, which is central to his role in the novel.
