# Film Studio gen pack (active addendum)

Apply these house rules when building clip video prompts:
- Dialogue clips must have audible speech and visible lip motion for the speaker; never silent mouths on dialogue.
- Continuity: when extending from previous clip, match wardrobe, place, and facing from the last frames.
- Respect VOICE LOCK and character visual locks exactly.
- Prefer tight action after speech; avoid long empty holds.
- Align performance energy with audio state. On silent or ambient-only clips: no roaring open mouth, no lip-sync shout, no screaming-at-camera performance. Restrained physical tension (clenched fist, tight jaw, white knuckles, held stare) is allowed and often preferred. On dialogue clips: mouth and gesture match the line and delivery; do not invent rage if the line is calm or measured.
- FACE / PERFORMANCE: State the intended facial expression and gaze for the beat. Match project PERFORMANCE rules (e.g. confessional address vs objective/observed). On dialogue: mouth and expression match the line and energy. On silent/ambient: no speech-performance open mouth; restrained tension is OK. When the beat needs a readable face, keep eyes open enough to read expression unless the plan explicitly calls for sleep, death, or covered eyes. Do not invent a default beauty-blank or wrong-emotion face. Avoid mismatched face: wrong emotion vs line, speech-mouth on silent, shut/averted eyes on address beats, or plastic unreadable face when identity is locked.
- STYLE MATCH (global): Match locked character refs and the project render style (picture-book CG, photoreal period, etc.). Do not swap or merge Character_* faces. Do not drift into a different medium mid-film (e.g. photoreal live-action when the project is stylized 3D, or plastic CGI faces when the project is photoreal). Project house rules name the exact medium.
- CAST COUNT: Honor any CAST COUNT line in the clip prompt — exact named on-screen identities only; no extra crowd or duplicate faces.
